Intel milestones

From invention to impact

Each milestone highlights a step toward smarter manufacturing, cleaner energy, and lasting environmental progress.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation and begin a long legacy of technological innovation.

This founding moment set the stage for future breakthroughs in computing.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel launches the 4004, the first commercial microprocessor, opening new possibilities for smaller, smarter devices.

A major step that helped transform technology into a daily tool for millions.

1978

8086 Processor

The 8086 establishes the x86 architecture that became a core foundation for personal computers and modern servers.

This standard shaped the future of software compatibility and computing.

1985

386 Processor

The 386 brings 32-bit performance and multitasking to personal computers, expanding what everyday users could accomplish.

A leap forward in power, efficiency, and the scale of digital technology.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel records its highest annual greenhouse gas emissions for operations before investing heavily in cleaner manufacturing.

The company used this turning point to drive stronger environmental action.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE strategy and 2030 goals focused on climate, water stewardship, and waste reduction across the business.

Sustainability became a central part of Intel's long-term roadmap.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel commits to net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across its global operations by 2040, building on years of environmental progress.

This target reflects a deeper commitment to measurable climate action.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel reaches 99% renewable electricity use worldwide, helping reduce emissions and support more responsible manufacturing practices.

A strong example of how clean energy investments can create real change.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit to connect suppliers, governments, and industry leaders around greener semiconductor manufacturing.

Collaboration is now a key part of Intel's sustainability story.
